Analysis

We were interested in seeing if any shift occurred during these two centuries. Thus, we counted the number of people per quarters and per "arrondissement". We then output two related graphs, normalized by the proportion of people of each century.

Here we can see something interesting. Indeed, we can notice a slight shift towards the "arrondissements" 16 and 17. What's interesting it that they are located outside the center. Our hypothesis is that, the venue of the subway facilitated the navigation within the city and hence one could travel farther distances more easily. This could explain this shift near the borders of the city.

This graph being more segmented, we can see more in details the shift observed above. A more detailed shift due to precise subway stations is really hard to quantify and would probably need a more advanced analysis.
